About the project

This project is my personal diary — full of reflections on the present, a search for beauty and silence in times of unrest.

The works presented here were created over the past few years, many of them during the ongoing war in Ukraine.

My years of study at the Faculty of Artistic Textiles and my fascination with threads inspired me to experiment by interweaving threads with pencil and paper. It is a laborious technique, far more demanding than I expected, yet it allowed me to realize my vision — works imbued with symbols and ethnocodes of our ancestors.

A new challenge, where each image emerges on its own, free from unnecessary associations, while I draw dreamily, immersing myself in the process and detaching from everything around me.

The project consists of more than 30 works.

Biography

I am a multidisciplinary artist from Ukraine, with a background rooted in textiles. After graduating from both the Lviv Art College and the Lviv Academy of Arts, my early work focused on experimenting with sculptural forms and Gobelin tapestries.

My artistic direction took a transformative turn in 2007 when I received the "Gaude Polonia" scholarship and studied at the Art Academy of Łódź, focusing on art textile workshops. This period of intense self-exploration pushed me toward painting and graphics, opening up a new realm of creative expression.

Today, I am focused on large-format original graphics and mixed-media textiles, combining my foundational knowledge of textiles with new techniques. My work reflects an ongoing search for self-expression and the interplay of materials, resulting in pieces that merge the tactile qualities of textiles with the expansive possibilities of painting and graphic art.

Artworks held in private collections across Ukraine, Poland, Canada, the USA, and Austria. Member of the National Union of Artists of Ukraine.
